# ****Low-Fidelity MVP Types****

If customers have a particular problem, it does not always mean that they are willing to solve it. That is why marketing research identifying customer needs is not enough to decide if your idea is worth developing. Low-fidelity MVPs help to define it more precisely.

### ****1. “The Fake Door” (= audience building MVP = An MVP before the MVP)****

A landing page that offers access to a feature you’d like to test. The CTA button itself guides you to the subscription page or checkout, only to lead to a message that this functionality is “under construction.”

The Fake Door” MVP can be more than just a landing page. It can be a button in your existing web app, a short link, or any other asset. Using this, you can collect emails of interested users to notify them when it becomes ready. It also quickly proves or disproves the tested idea. However it could potentially decrease your credibility, as some customers perceive such experiments as a scam. It also Implies lack of insights and distortion in results, as many users may interact with the ‘fake door’ for the sake of curiosity.

If you already have a customer base, “The Fake Door” MVP is a suitable tool to determine if they might be interested in a new feature or product. 

### ****2. Landing page MVP****

It resembles the “The Fake Door” approach. However, you do not need to pretend that a product already exists before leading a user to a “coming soon” message. It is relatively inexpensive and easy to deploy. But you cannot analyze what made people turn away. It may be useful if you need to get an initial customer base for product creation to define the content and features of the future solution.

### ****3. Email Campaign MVP****

It is useful when you’d like to determine whether your customers are interested in a new feature or product among an existing customer base. It works best in tandem with a landing page MVP or other approaches as a follow-up step. It allows direct interaction with each customer but requires having an email base of target users.

### ****4. Marketing Campaign MVP****

To investigate your target audience, you can use all free and paid promotional methods available today. It is great for running split tests for obtaining comprehensive marketing statistics. It is an excellent tool for testing ideas and learning the target market and is great for running split tests for obtaining comprehensive marketing statistics.

# ****High-Fidelity MVP Types****

High-Fidelity MVP types helps detect the directions for further development and engage first adopters. 

### 5. Single-Feature MVP

This is the most traditional option usually used to explain what an MVP is. If you are sure of the core functionality of your future product, implement it in a single-feature MVP to check its viability on the actual market. 

Typically, the “single-feature” approach has a narrower target audience. Such product MVPs also receive multiple user complaints due to the limited functionality. However, if no complaints are received, users are not ready to pay for add-ons, or the product is not attractive.

### ****6. Pre-order MVP****

It is a solution that describes the future product and engages consumers to pay for it before its actual release. With its help, you can gain early adopters and get funds for further product development. it gives less feedback than an ordinary “sign up for updates” landing page MVP. Unless you have a reputation of a world-renowned successful business owner, people might feel suspicious when paying for a non-existent product.

### ****7. Concierge MVP****

It is a product that simulates a multifunctional software solution while a human fulfills its primary functions.

### ****8. Wizard of Oz MVP****

The offered features are processed and executed manually. And unlike Concierge MVP, users do not know about it. It requires detailed behavior instructions for different reactions and situations occurring while servicing users.

### ****9. Piecemeal MVP****

The idea of this MVP is to use existing tools and solutions for providing your product features.
