## High-fidelity MVP

For understanding if the customers are ready to pay for your solution

### High-fidelity MVP is used for:

- Defining and optimizing a marketing strategy
- Testing the value proposition, communication channels, and CTA
- Defining potential strategies for growth
- Acquiring early adopters and asserting a product on the market
- Learning the demand in the product

### Concierge MVP

a product that uses manual guiding of users instead of an automated algorithm

### Wizard of OZ MVP ( = [Mechanical Turk MVP](https://aws.amazon.com/ko/premiumsupport/knowledge-center/mechanical-turk-use-cases/))

a product that uses the human-powered functionality, but looks and feels like a real solution

> [Mechanical Turk](http://mechanicalturk.com/) is a great system for outsourcing repetitive, tedious tasks. What makes it particularly interesting for startups is that human power has become super inexpensive, practical and easy to use. For Lean Startups this is extremely useful. A great example of a "mechanical turk mvp" (or Pretotype) is [IBM's test of a Speech-to-Text Technology](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=t4AqxNekecY&t=962s).
> 

### [Piecemeal MVP](https://medium.com/@mdotflow22/types-of-minimum-viable-products-series-the-single-feature-application-e3c7e182a2bb)

a product whose significant piece of functionality is powered manually, but some of its missing features are emulated with existing services. This is the most traditional option usually used to explain what an MVP is.

Steve Blank advised a startup aimed at producing and selling [hyper-spectral images](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hyperspectral_imaging) captured by Unmanned Aerial Vehicles (drones) with a [Hyper-spectral camera](http://www.microimages.com/documentation/Tutorials/hyprspec.pdf) flown over the farm fields. His MVP was to use an HD camera and a helicopter to collect the images and sell them.

### Single-Featured / One Painkiller Feature MVP

You develop a simple app that solves a single, specific problem your customers have. The feature you provide should be core to your unique value proposition. By focusing on a single feature, you can minimize expenses and time spent creating an application. But, a single feature MVP requires you to build a functioning app, which requires technical expertise (vs. creating a landing page or email campaign).

The first Spotify prototype was up and running in under five months and launched as a beta streaming service in 2007. The only feature that people were able to use: stream single songs. As time went on and the subscriber base grew, Spotify carefully chose apps to partner with, features to add, and cross-platform support to roll out.

## Low-Fidelity MVP

For understanding the customer’s problem to find a suitable solution

### Low-fidelity MVP is used for:

- Learning the practical value of the product
- Finding out the most effective solutions the customers are in need of
- Getting a better understanding of customer's needs
- Niche analysis - whether or not the problem is worth solving

### Types of low-fidelity MVPs:

- Blogs
- Forums / communities
- Surveys / questionnaires
- Landing pages
- Explainer videos
- Advertising campaigns
- Mockups / slide decks / presentations
- Crowdfunding campaigns
- Idea spotting networks
